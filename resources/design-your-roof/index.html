Compare roof systems

Tile roofing

Good for Arizona style and heat, but underlayment, valleys, and broken tiles need careful review.

Shingle roofing

A practical residential option where ventilation, heat exposure, flashing, and storm wear affect life span.

Foam and flat roofing

Strong low-slope options when drainage, coating condition, and recoat timing are managed correctly.

Metal roofing

Durable in the desert when seams, fasteners, edges, and penetrations are inspected and maintained.

Questions to answer before choosing

What is the roof slope?

Flat and low-slope sections need different systems than steep shingle or tile areas.

What is failing?

Underlayment, coating, flashing, fasteners, and surface material call for different fixes.

What does the neighborhood require?

HOA expectations, color, profile, and visible roof style can affect our final recommendation.

How long will you own the home?

A short-term repair and a long-term replacement may both be valid, depending on your plan.

What has leaked before?

Past leak history helps us focus the inspection on valleys, penetrations, transitions, and drainage.

What proof do you need?

Photos and a written scope help compare estimates without pressure.
