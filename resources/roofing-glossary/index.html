Common estimate terms

Underlayment

The protective layer beneath tile that often matters more than the tile surface itself.

Flashing

Metal or roof detail used around walls, edges, chimneys, and transitions to control water.

Penetration

Anything passing through the roof surface, such as vents, pipes, skylights, or equipment.

Valley

The area where roof planes meet and water concentrates during rain.

Coating

The protective top layer used on foam and some flat roof systems.

Decking

The structural roof surface below the roofing assembly.

Fastener

Screws, nails, or other attachments that can loosen or age over time.

Ridge

The high line where two roof planes meet.

Scope

The written description of what work is included, excluded, and priced.

Why these terms matter

When a contractor says the problem is flashing, underlayment, coating, or decking, the estimate should connect that term to a specific roof condition photo.
